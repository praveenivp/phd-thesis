\section{Deuterium Metabolic Imaging}
Another high-field methodology which can greatly benefit from the SNR improvements of bSSFP is \gls{DMI}. Being able to detect metabolic changes is a powerful clinical tool, especially in the field of oncology for diagnosis and treatment monitoring.  




Presently, the clinical diagnosis of brain tumors are predominately based on contrast \gls{MRI} and \gls{CT} which has the highest sensitivity to detect aggressive tumor  \gls{BBB} damage. The molecular imaging like \gls{PET} helps mainly with the tumor staging and treatment monitoring. Especially, \gls{PET} with [$^{18}$F]flurodeoxyglucose (FDG) is most common 



\subsection{tumor metabolism}
In order to maintain normal cellular integrity and integrity, all cells require energy in the form of adenosine triphosphate (ATP) which it obtains mainly through glucose metabolism. The glucose metabolism begins with the glucose being transported from the blood into the cell by specialized membrane proteins and undergoes glycolysis in the cytoplasm to produce pyruvate. In the healthy cells, pyruvate enter the tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle and oxidative phosphorylation in the mitochondria for the most efficient ATP production as illustrated in \cref{fig:dmi_intro}. In tumor cells, however, the anaerobic fermentation of pyruvate into lactic acid is upregulated. Even though this process is inefficient, it is 10-100 times faster than mitochondrial respiration and provide additional bio-materials for rapid cell division\cite{libertiWarburgEffectHow2016}. This results in increased glucose consumption of the turmor cells known as the Warburg effect\cite{warburgOriginCancerCells1956}. The Warburg effect is an important cancer biomarker used  in all modern molecular imaging modality such as \gls{PET}~\cite{reivich18FfluorodeoxyglucoseMethodMeasurement1979, \gls{DMI}~\cite{defeyterDeuteriumMetabolicImaging2021}, hyper-polarized $^{13}C$- \gls{MRI}~\cite{gordonTranslationCarbon13EPI2019} and Gluco\gls{CEST}~\cite{capozzaGlucoCESTMRIEvaluation2022}
